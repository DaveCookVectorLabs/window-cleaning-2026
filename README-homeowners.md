## What This Does

You enter some basic information about your home's windows and this calculator gives you a realistic cost estimate for professional window cleaning. No signup, no email capture, no sales pitch — just math.

**Inputs:**

- How many windows your home has
- Whether you want interior, exterior, or both cleaned
- Whether any windows require ladder access (second storey, dormers, skylights)
- How often you plan to have them cleaned

**Outputs:**

- Estimated total cost
- Per-window cost breakdown
- What you'd save on a recurring schedule vs. one-time cleaning

The calculator uses the same pricing variables that professional cleaning companies use internally. The goal is to give you a number you can compare against actual quotes.

## How Much Does Window Cleaning Cost?

This is the question every homeowner asks, and the answer is always "it depends." But here's what it actually depends on, and roughly what the numbers look like in Northern Ontario.

### Window Count

Most homes in North Bay and Sudbury have between 12 and 25 windows. A standard three-bedroom bungalow might have 14 to 16 windows. A two-storey four-bedroom home might have 20 to 28. Every window adds to the time a technician spends on your home, so window count is the starting point for any quote.

### Interior vs. Exterior vs. Both

Exterior-only cleaning is faster because the technician isn't working around furniture, blinds, or curtains. Interior cleaning requires more care — shoe covers, drop cloths, working around your belongings. A full-service clean (interior + exterior + screens + tracks) typically costs 40% to 60% more than exterior-only.

### Ground Level vs. Ladder Access

If all your windows are reachable from ground level with an extension pole, that's the simplest and cheapest scenario. Second-storey windows that require ladder work add time and cost. Dormers, skylights, and hard-to-reach windows above rooflines add more.

For most single-storey homes in Northern Ontario, professional window cleaning runs between $150 and $300 for a full-service clean (interior + exterior + screens + tracks). Two-storey homes with ladder access typically run $250 to $500 depending on window count and accessibility.

### Seasonal Timing

In Northern Ontario, exterior window cleaning is practical from roughly April through November. The best times to book are late spring (after winter salt spray and road grime have done their damage) and early fall (before freeze-up). Most homeowners book one or two full cleans per year — spring and fall — which keeps glass clear and prevents mineral buildup from becoming permanent.

Winter builds up a surprising amount of grime on exterior glass. Road salt spray, wood smoke residue, and freeze-thaw cycles leave mineral deposits that get harder to remove the longer they sit. A spring clean after winter is the single highest-impact window cleaning you can schedule all year.

## What a Professional Window Clean Actually Includes

When you hire a professional cleaning company (not a guy with a squeegee and a bucket off Kijiji), a standard residential window clean should include:

- **All interior glass** — every window in the home, streak-free
- **All exterior glass** — ground level and second storey
- **Screen removal, cleaning, and reinstallation** — screens get dusty and trap pollen; cleaning them makes a visible difference
- **Track and sill wiping** — dirt, dead insects, and debris accumulate in window tracks year-round
- **Sliding and patio door glass** — often forgotten but included in a proper service
- **Fragrance-free products on request** — important for households with sensitivities or allergies

If a company quotes you for "window cleaning" and doesn't include screens and tracks, they're quoting you for glass-only, and you'll notice the difference.

## How to Choose a Window Cleaning Company

A few things to look for, especially in Ontario:

**WSIB coverage.** Any company sending workers into your home in Ontario should carry WSIB (Workplace Safety and Insurance Board) coverage. If an uninsured worker gets injured on your property, you could be liable. Ask for proof.

**Liability insurance.** Accidents happen — a ladder scratches siding, a squeegee nicks a window seal. A properly insured company carries commercial general liability coverage that protects your property.

**Consistent crews.** You're letting people into your home. A company that sends the same team each visit is a company that takes residential work seriously. High turnover and random crews are a red flag.

**Transparent pricing.** You should be able to get a quote before anyone shows up. If a company won't give you a number without an in-person visit for a standard residential window clean, they're either going to upsell you on-site or they don't have their pricing figured out.
